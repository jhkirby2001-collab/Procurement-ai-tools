## Critical Problem Areas Analysis Report

**Report Date:** January 8, 2026

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The City of Chicago has **$68.9 million** in procurement spending across **1,112 requisitions** that are operating without active contracts. This analysis identifies the most critical problem areas requiring immediate attention.

### Key Findings:
- **80.4%** ($55.3M) is due to expired contracts with new contracts pending
- **84.2%** ($58M) of requisitions have NO contract awarded yet
- Average requisition has been pending for **189 days** (6+ months)
- Top 20 vendors account for **$48.7M** (70.7%) of at-risk spending
- **3 departments** account for **58.6%** of all at-risk spending

---

## CRITICAL PROBLEM AREA #1: EXPIRED CONTRACTS - NEW CONTRACTS PENDING
### $55.3 Million | 860 Requisitions

**IMPACT:** This is the single largest compliance issue, representing 80.4% of all at-risk spending.

**ROOT CAUSE:** Departments continue to purchase from vendors whose contracts have expired while waiting for new contract awards.

**WHY THIS IS CRITICAL:**
- Departments have no contractual protections
- Pricing may not be competitive or pre-negotiated
- Legal exposure for the city
- No performance guarantees or SLAs

**RECOMMENDED ACTIONS:**
1. Implement 90-day pre-expiration alerts for all contracts
2. Start renewal/rebid process 180 days before expiration
3. Create emergency bridge contract procedures
4. Track and report monthly on at-risk expirations
