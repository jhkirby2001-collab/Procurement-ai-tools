## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The City of Chicago is wasting **$389,668.79** annually on bottled water purchases despite managing one of the finest municipal water systems in the United States. This analysis reveals systematic fiscal irresponsibility and provides a clear path to save **$363,690.87** per year through innovation and sustainable practices with a payback period of only **3.3 months**.

### Key Findings:
- **96 bottled water purchases** identified in one year
- **259,779 gallons** of bottled water purchased
- **67,543 plastic bottles/jugs** waste per year
- **3,377 pounds** of plastic waste generated
- **Chicago Fire Department** is the worst offender spending **$307,105.19**

## RECOMMENDATIONS FOR IMMEDIATE ACTION

### 1. IMPLEMENT CITYWIDE BOTTLED WATER BAN
**Action:** Prohibit all bottled water purchases city-wide
- Effective immediately (30-day implementation)
- Exceptions only for verified emergencies
- Expected annual savings: **$363,690.87**

### 2. INSTALL WATER FILTRATION INFRASTRUCTURE
**Action:** Deploy modern filtration and bottle refill stations
- Install in all city buildings (prioritize Fire Dept locations)
- Provide reusable bottles to 10,000+ city employees
- Partner with local manufacturers for bottles
- Investment: **$100,000.00**
- Payback period: **3.3 months**

### 3. LEVERAGE CHICAGO'S MUNICIPAL WATER SYSTEM
**Action:** Connect all water coolers to building water supply
- Chicago has award-winning municipal water quality
- Lake Michigan provides excellent source water
- Existing infrastructure in all city buildings
- Near-zero ongoing cost

### 4. PRIORITY IMPLEMENTATION BY DEPARTMENT

**PHASE 1: Chicago Fire Department (Immediate)**
- Current waste: **$307,105.19** annually (79% of total)
- Single purchase of **$108,111.88** in February 2025
- Deploy filtration systems to all fire stations
- Provide reusable bottles to all firefighters
- Potential savings: **$290,000+** per year

**PHASE 2: Family & Support Services**
- Current waste: **$32,536.00** annually
- Model sustainable practices for communities served
- Demonstrate fiscal responsibility

**PHASE 3: Aviation, Streets & Sanitation, and Others**
- Remaining **$50,027.60** in annual waste
- Scale infrastructure investment

### 5. ESTABLISH ACCOUNTABILITY MEASURES
- Monthly bottled water spending reports to City Council
- Department heads must justify any water purchases in writing
- Quarterly sustainability and cost reduction reviews
- Celebrate departments achieving zero bottled water purchases
- Tie departmental performance metrics to sustainability goals

### 6. PUBLIC COMMUNICATION STRATEGY
**Turn waste reduction into positive PR:**
- "Chicago Saves $363K Annually While Going Green"
- Lead by example for residents and businesses
- Align with climate action commitments
- Generate positive media coverage
- Demonstrate fiscal responsibility to taxpayers

## CONCLUSION

The City of Chicago is wasting **$389,668.79** annually on bottled water—a completely avoidable expense given Chicago's world-class municipal water infrastructure and award-winning water quality. This waste represents:

1. **Fiscal Irresponsibility**
   Taxpayer money spent on recurring expenses with no asset creation

2. **Environmental Harm**
   67,543 plastic bottles sent to landfills annually, generating 3,377 lbs of plastic waste

3. **Institutional Hypocrisy**
   City promotes sustainability publicly while creating plastic waste internally

4. **Missed Savings Opportunity**
   $363,690.87 annual savings available with 3.3-month payback period

By implementing the recommendations in this report, Chicago can:
- Save **$363,690.87 per year** starting Year 1
